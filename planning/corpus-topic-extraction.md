## Through-Lines Across the Corpus

These themes appear in 4+ articles and represent Josh's consistent editorial voice (9 articles total):

1. **Product judgment matters more than technical capability.** Every article argues that the hard decisions are about what to build, for whom, and when to stop — not about the code itself. (NYC Schools, Labor Market BTC, Flywheel, AI Playbook, Escape Velocity)

2. **AI is a collaborator, not a replacement — but that requires active interrogation.** Uncritical use of AI produces mediocre results. The value comes from pushing back, redirecting, and maintaining editorial judgment. (Escape Velocity, Labor Market BTC, NYC Schools, Sports Highlights)

3. **Experience at media companies informs everything.** Nearly every piece references NBC News Group, newsroom workflows, editorial processes, or media industry dynamics. This isn't casual name-dropping — it's the earned wisdom that grounds the arguments. (All 9 articles)

4. **Show the work, including the failures.** The kill-your-darlings moments (Labor Market dashboard → article pivot, Sports Highlights video frame failures, Escape Velocity's self-critique) are structural features of the writing, not embarrassing admissions. (Labor Market BTC, Sports Highlights, Escape Velocity, Playing the Game Well)

5. **Frameworks from practice, not theory.** The Whole Product Flywheel, the Shape Up extensions, the behavior matrix, the four leadership videos — these emerged from actual work in actual organizations, not academic synthesis. (Flywheel, AI Playbook, Playing the Game Well, Mindset)

6. **Leadership communicated through sports and cultural storytelling.** Giannis on failure, Kara Lawson on hard things, Bruce Lee on adaptability, Fred Rogers on helping. This is a distinctive voice element — using non-business cultural references to communicate leadership values. Not many product leaders write this way. (Mindset, Playing the Game Well, Sports Highlights)
